Infrastructure

  • WORLD CLASS INFRASTRUCTURE

    HISTORIC PARTNERSHIPS WITH NASA STENNIS SPACE CENTER AND THE U.S. AIR FORCE

    Relativity operates multiple production, test, and launch sites across the United States, enabling shorter lead times to launch, and greater launch flexibility for commercial and government customers needing access to space.

  • THE WORMHOLE

    LONG BEACH, CALIFORNIA

    As a 93-acre former Boeing C-17 manufacturing plant in Long Beach, Relativity headquarters, called “The Wormhole", is one of the largest headquarters in the private space industry.

    • 1 million sq. ft.
    • 100 ft. fall ceilings
    • Capacity for 2,000+ employees, metallurgical laboratory, powder bed fusion (PBF) printers, mission control center, as well as dozens of the company’s proprietary Stargate 3D printers, the largest metal 3D printers in the world.
    • Focused on production for Terran R, Relativity’s fully reusable, 3D printed rocket.

  • THE PORTAL

    LONG BEACH, CALIFORNIA

    This 120,000 sq. ft. factory hosts business operations and production for Terran 1, the world’s first 3D printed rocket.

  • E2 Test Facility

    STENNIS SPACE CENTER, MISSISSIPPI

    Development, qualification, and acceptance testing of Aeon 1 and Aeon R engines occurs at the E2 Test Complex. Relativity is investing capital to build on the existing site with a permanent team leading test operations.

    • 25 acre test complex including specialized, ultra-high pressure fluid systems rated for up to 15,000 psig
    • Two large test cells rated for horizontally-mounted engines and for vertically-mounted vehicle stages and/or engines
    • Up to 324,000 lbs of thrust
    • Aeon R components are currently in testing at E2, with plans to build the first Aeon R engine and turbopump assembly and begin thrust chamber assembly tests for Aeon R engines in the coming months at a leased E1 test cell.

  • E4 TEST FACILITY

    STENNIS SPACE CENTER, MISSISSIPPI

    Complete development, qualification, and acceptance testing of Terran 1 and Aeon engines occurs at a dual-bay test stand at E4 Test Complex (E4A and E4B). Relativity built on the existing site with a permanent team leading test operations.

    • 25 acre test complex including 15,000 ft2 of specialized test infrastructure
    • Four large test cells rated for both stage and engine testing
    • Exclusive 20 year agreement - Stennis' first ever Commercial Space Launch Act

  • AEON R TEST COMPLEX

    STENNIS SPACE CENTER, MISSISSIPPI

    Relativity will utilize over 150 additional acres within the Stennis Test Complex, with testing infrastructure for its Aeon R engines being built from the ground up on previously unutilized land north of the A and E Test Complexes.

    • New facilities include new engine test stands, a full scale second stage stand, office buildings, and a vehicle hangar.
    • Full Aeon R engine tests are tracking to occur in late 2023.

  • STENNIS FACTORY

    STENNIS SPACE CENTER, MISSISSIPPI

    Relativity secured a 20-year exclusive use agreement for a 220,000 sq. ft. factory building at NASA Stennis Space Center to house one of our Stargate factories and for faster development of its Terran rockets. With production and testing facilities on one site, Relativity can offer greater flexibility to our customers.

    • 80-foot high bay
    • 50-ton overhead crane
    • Existing industrial infrastructure
    • 2 miles from Relativity's test sites

  • ENGINEERING OFFICE

    SEATTLE, WASHINGTON

    Originally founded in a co-working space in Seattle, Relativity’s current Seattle office houses a portion of the company’s propulsion team.

  • Building 330 and Adjacent Land

    VANDENBERG AIR FORCE BASE, CALIFORNIA

    Relativity has a Right of Entry Agreement with the United States Air Force, 30th Space Wing, for development of rocket launch facilities at Vandenberg Air Force Base. Relativity’s launch capabilities will offer a complete range of orbital inclinations, including polar and sun synchronous orbits (SSO).

  • LAUNCH COMPLEX 16

    CAPE CANAVERAL, FLORIDA

    Relativity is the first and only venture-backed company with a Right of Entry directly with the U.S. Air Force at historic Launch Complex 16.

    • Launch Pad, Propellant Farms and Gas Storage
    • Integration Hangar & Logistics Area
    • Payload Processing Facility (PPF)
    • Launch Control Center (LCC)
